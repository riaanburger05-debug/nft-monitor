The 3 Critical Rules

⚙️
Critical
Pump runs 24/7 — no exceptions
NFT roots are always exposed to air. A stopped pump means dry roots within minutes. Always keep a spare pump in stock.
💀 Without flow: roots die in under 30 minutes. No recovery possible.
🧪
Critical
Change EC or pH — never both at once
Adjust one parameter, wait 30 minutes, then re-measure. Changing both at once makes it impossible to know what worked — and risks overcorrection.
⚠️ Dual correction often overshoots in both directions, stressing plants further.
💧
Critical
Never let the tank run empty
An empty tank means the pump runs dry, overheats and burns out — usually mid-night. Check water levels every morning without fail.
💀 Dry pump = burned motor + no flow = dead roots. Two failures at once.
✅

Always Do This

📏
Setup
Maintain 1–2% pipe slope
Pipes must slope slightly downward so water flows by gravity. No slope = water pools = root rot and uneven feeding.
📐 Use a spirit level when first installing pipes.
☀️
Setup
Use white or light-coloured pipes
Dark pipes absorb heat and raise water temperature. They also encourage algae growth inside the pipe where light leaks in.
🌡️ Water above 24°C loses oxygen — roots suffocate.
🪣
Routine
Cover the reservoir tank
An uncovered tank lets in light, causing algae blooms. It also increases evaporation and allows debris to fall in and contaminate the solution.
🌿 Algae competes with plants for nutrients and clogs pumps.
🔬
Important
Check tap water EC first
Tap water already contains dissolved minerals. Your starting EC is not zero. Always measure tap water before mixing nutrients.
⚡ High tap EC means less nutrient to add than the recipe says.
🛠️
Routine
Flush & clean tanks every 4–6 weeks
Salt build-up distorts EC readings over time. Biofilm on tank walls harbours pathogens. Drain, scrub with white vinegar, rinse and refill.
🧹 Always clean between crop cycles — never skip this step.
📓
Important
Log EC & pH readings daily
A single reading tells you the current state. A log tells you the trend — whether things are drifting, improving or getting worse week by week.
📈 Trends catch problems before plants show symptoms.
❌

Never Do This

🌱
Critical
Don't fully submerge roots
NFT requires a thin film of water only. Roots must have air above the film. Full submersion cuts off oxygen and causes root rot within days.
💧 Thin film = water + oxygen. Flooded pipe = oxygen = zero.
🪵
Critical
Don't let roots block the drain
Vigorous roots can grow into the drain opening and back up water in the pipe. Check drain openings weekly, especially with fast-growing crops.
🔍 Weekly inspection saves a flooded pipe.
⚗️
Important
Don't mix concentrates undiluted
Nutrient Part A and Part B must never be mixed together in concentrated form. They react, precipitate and become unavailable to plants.
✅ Always add A to water first, stir, then add B separately.
💊
Important
Don't adjust pH in large doses
pH Up and pH Down are powerful. A large dose swings the pH past the target and stresses plants. Add a little, wait, then re-measure before adding more.
⏱️ Wait 10–15 minutes after each addition before measuring again.
🍅
Setup
Don't grow heavy crops without support
Tomatoes, peppers and cucumbers produce large root masses that block flow and heavy fruit that tips pipes. They need structural support and wider pipes.
✅ NFT works best with leafy greens, herbs and strawberries.
🍂
Routine
Don't skip cleaning between cycles
Old root matter left in pipes decomposes, feeds Pythium fungus and introduces it to the next crop. Always remove all root material and disinfect before replanting.
🧼 Use diluted hydrogen peroxide to sterilise pipes between cycles.
⚠️

Warning Signs — Act Immediately

🟤
Brown, slimy roots
Root Rot — Pythium
Drain the tank immediately. Remove affected plants. Disinfect with diluted H₂O₂. Improve water temperature and oxygen levels before replanting.
🟡
EC rising but plants look weak or yellow
pH Problem, Not EC
When pH is wrong, plants cannot absorb nutrients even if EC is perfect. Always fix pH first before adjusting EC. Re-check pH after any EC change.
📉
EC dropping faster than expected
Heavy Feeding or Rapid Evaporation
Check tank water level first — low level concentrates or dilutes readings. If level is fine, plants are feeding heavily. Top up with mixed nutrient solution, not plain water.
🟢
Green coating inside pipes or on tank walls
Algae Bloom
Light is entering the system. Immediately cover all gaps in pipes, caps and tank lid. Clean affected surfaces with diluted bleach solution and rinse thoroughly.
